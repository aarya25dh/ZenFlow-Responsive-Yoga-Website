Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our classes, membership, and services

Do I need prior yoga experience to join? +

Not at all! Our classes are designed for all levels - from complete beginners to advanced practitioners. We recommend starting with our beginner-level Hatha or gentle Yin classes. Our instructors will provide modifications for every pose. We offer a free trial class so you can experience the studio and ask any questions!

Do you teach in English? Can international travelers join? +

Yes! All our classes are taught in English, making them perfect for international tourists and travelers visiting Kathmandu. Our instructors are fluent English speakers experienced with students from around the world. Walk-ins are welcome, and our beginner classes are especially suited for first-time visitors. We're located in Thamel, the heart of the tourist area!

How long is each class? +

Most of our classes are 60 minutes long, which includes warm-up, main practice, and cool-down/savasana. We also offer 30-minute express classes during lunch hours and 90-minute immersive sessions on certain days. Check our schedule for specific durations.

What about altitude? Will high altitude affect my practice? +

Kathmandu sits at 1,338m (4,390 ft) elevation. Most visitors and locals adjust within a few days. Our instructors are trained to guide you through altitude adaptation. We recommend drinking plenty of water, starting with gentler classes like Hatha or Yin if you're new to the altitude, and taking breaks as needed. Many find that yoga actually helps with acclimatization!

What if I'm pregnant or have injuries? +

Please inform your instructor before class begins. We offer prenatal yoga classes for expecting mothers and have instructors trained in modifications for various injuries and conditions. We're happy to customize your practice. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Do you offer private sessions? +

Yes! We offer personalized one-on-one sessions tailored to your goals and needs. Private sessions are available for beginners, athletes, rehabilitation, or anyone seeking focused attention. Contact us for rates and scheduling.

What are your pricing options? Do you offer tourist packages? +

We offer flexible pricing in Nepali Rupees (NPR): Drop-in class NPR 800, Weekly pass NPR 3,500, Monthly unlimited NPR 8,500, and special Tourist 5-Day Pass NPR 4,000. We also offer private sessions, workshops, and group bookings. Payment accepted in cash (NPR) or through major mobile payment services. Contact us for current rates and special group discounts.

Do you offer corporate wellness programs? +

Absolutely! We offer customized corporate wellness programs for companies of all sizes. Options include on-site classes, group memberships, wellness workshops, and stress-reduction seminars. Bulk discounts available. Email support@zenflow.com for details.

Is your studio connected to Buddhist or spiritual traditions? +

Absolutely! ZenFlow Nepal is deeply rooted in Himalayan yoga traditions and inspired by Buddhist meditation practices found throughout Nepal. We're walking distance from Boudhanath (UNESCO World Heritage Site) and honor the spiritual heritage of Lumbini, birthplace of Buddha. Our practice blends ancient wisdom with modern wellness. You'll find our classes infused with mindfulness, pranayama, and meditation that align with Nepal's rich spiritual legacy.

Do you offer workshops or special events? +

Yes! We regularly offer special workshops, seasonal celebrations, and events aligned with Nepal's festivals and spiritual calendar. Recent offerings include Himalayan meditation retreats, pranayama intensives, and sunrise rooftop sessions with views of the Kathmandu valley. We also host visiting teachers and offer themed yoga retreats to sacred sites like Pashupatinath Temple. Follow our social media for announcements!
